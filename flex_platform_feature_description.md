# Flex Platform — Feature Overview



## Platform Positioning

Flex is a data-driven energy optimisation platform designed to maximise the value of distributed energy assets.
It enables businesses to move beyond monitoring, providing the tools to understand, optimise, and actively manage energy performance behind the meter. By combining real-time data, financial analysis, and operational control, Flex helps customers unlock more value from their solar, battery, and energy infrastructure.
- Flex:Assure → Is my system delivering what was promised?
- Flex:Insights → What operational improvements should I make?
- Flex:Tariff → Am I on the right pricing structure?
- Flex:Simulator → What would have happened if I operated differently?
- Flex:Schedule → Give me simple control over how the system runs
- Flex:Optimisation → Automatically operate the system for the best outcome
- Flex:Submetering → Show me where energy is being used across the site
- Flex:Reporting → Keep me informed with clear, ongoing performance updates

## Flex:Insights


### Headline

Turn energy data into clear, actionable decisions.

### High-level explainer

Flex:Insights analyses your energy data and provides practical recommendations to improve system performance and financial outcomes.

### Detailed explainer

Energy data on its own has limited value. Most platforms stop at showing charts, trends, and raw metrics, leaving the customer to interpret what it all means and what actions should be taken.
Flex:Insights is designed to bridge that gap. It continuously analyses site-specific energy behaviour, including consumption patterns, solar generation, import and export activity, and, where applicable, battery performance. It then translates this information into clear, practical recommendations that can be acted on.
Rather than providing generic advice, these insights are grounded in how the site actually operates. The platform identifies recurring patterns such as early-morning load spikes, underutilised solar generation windows, or inefficient alignment between energy demand and generation. It highlights opportunities to improve outcomes by adjusting how energy is used, when it is used, and how it interacts with the system.

Importantly, Flex:Insights ties these recommendations to financial outcomes wherever possible. This means users can understand not just what to change, but why it matters in dollar terms. For example, shifting load into solar hours is not just presented as a technical improvement—it is framed as a reduction in grid import cost or an increase in solar utilisation value.
Over time, Flex:Insights becomes a continuous improvement engine. As site behaviour changes, new equipment is added, or operating patterns evolve, the platform continues to identify new opportunities. This ensures that optimisation is not a one-off exercise, but an ongoing process that adapts with the customer.

### Key capabilities

- Identification of site-specific energy usage patterns
- Data-driven operational recommendations based on real behaviour
- Load shifting insights aligned with solar generation windows
- Self-consumption improvement opportunities
- Identification of peak demand and inefficient usage periods
- Quantification of financial impact for recommended actions
- Continuous monitoring and surfacing of new optimisation opportunities

### Why it matters

Most customers do not have the time, expertise, or tools to interpret detailed energy data and translate it into meaningful action.
Flex:Insights simplifies this process. It ensures that the value hidden within energy data is surfaced and converted into practical, commercially relevant decisions. This helps customers focus on the actions that will have the greatest impact on cost, savings, and system performance.

### Example use case

A farm has significant electricity usage in the early morning for hot water heating and pumping, before meaningful solar generation begins.
Flex:Insights identifies this pattern and recommends shifting these loads into the late morning and early afternoon solar window, where solar generation is highest.

### The recommendation includes a quantified estimate showing:

- Increased solar self-consumption
- Reduced grid imports during non-solar hours
- Improved utilisation of existing solar generation

### Result:

The customer is able to make a simple operational change that improves both energy efficiency and financial performance.

### Outcome

- Smarter operational decisions
- Increased value from existing energy assets
- Reduced energy costs through behavioural change
- Continuous performance improvement driven by real data

## Flex:Simulator


### Headline

Test energy strategies. See the financial outcome—before you act.

### High-level explainer

Flex:Simulator allows you to model alternative energy strategies using your own historical data, so you can understand the real financial impact of changing how your system operates.

### Detailed explainer

Energy optimisation often involves trial and error. Without the ability to test changes in advance, customers must rely on assumptions when adjusting battery schedules, export strategies, or tariff responses.
Flex:Simulator removes this uncertainty by enabling true backtesting. It takes the customer’s actual historical energy data—including interval consumption, solar generation, import and export flows, and tariff structures—and replays that data under alternative operating scenarios.
This allows users to ask “what if” questions and receive answers grounded in real performance data rather than theoretical models.
For example, a user can test what would have happened if the battery charged later in the day, if discharge was reserved for peak pricing periods, or if export behaviour was adjusted to capture higher-value price events. The platform calculates how those changes would have impacted both energy flows and financial outcomes over the selected period.
Because the simulation is based on real site data, the outputs are highly relevant and specific to the customer. This makes it possible to identify missed value, validate new strategies, and build confidence before making changes in a live environment.
Flex:Simulator also plays a critical role in the broader Flex platform. It provides the analytical foundation that supports optimisation, enabling customers to move from insight to action with confidence.

### Key capabilities

- Scenario modelling using real historical site data
- Simulation of alternative battery charge and discharge strategies
- Testing of different import, export, and tariff approaches
- Replay of historical energy data under new operating rules
- Financial comparison of actual vs simulated outcomes
- Side-by-side comparison of multiple scenarios
- Identification of missed value and optimisation opportunities

### Why it matters

Without backtesting, optimisation decisions are often based on guesswork.
Flex:Simulator allows customers to quantify the impact of different strategies before implementing them. This reduces risk, improves confidence, and ensures that changes are driven by evidence rather than assumptions.

### Example use case

A commercial customer wants to improve the performance of their battery system.
Using Flex:Simulator, they replay the previous month and test an alternative strategy:
- Charging later in the solar window
- Preserving battery energy for peak pricing periods
The simulation shows:
- Reduced grid import during high-cost periods
- Increased export revenue
- Improved overall financial outcome

### Result:

The customer can clearly see the value of adjusting their operating strategy before implementing it.

### Outcome

- Evidence-based decision making
- Clear understanding of optimisation opportunities
- Reduced risk when changing system behaviour
- Improved financial performance without additional hardware

## Flex:Schedule


### Headline

Take control with simple, rule-based energy automation.

### High-level explainer

Flex:Schedule allows you to define when and how your energy system operates using simple, configurable rules.

### Detailed explainer

Not every customer requires advanced optimisation from day one. In many cases, a simple and predictable control strategy is the most effective way to improve system performance.
Flex:Schedule provides a straightforward way to control battery behaviour using time-based rules and defined operating preferences. Users can specify when the battery charges, when it discharges, and how it interacts with the grid.
This allows alignment with known site behaviour, such as solar generation windows, peak pricing periods, or operational requirements. For example, a customer may choose to charge the battery during peak solar hours and discharge during high-cost evening periods.
Flex:Schedule also acts as the foundation for more advanced functionality. It enables customers to implement and test basic strategies before moving to simulation or automated optimisation. This staged approach helps customers build confidence and progressively unlock more value from their system.

### Key capabilities

- Time-based battery charge and discharge scheduling
- Rule-based control logic for energy assets
- Alignment with tariff windows and operational needs
- Export and grid interaction preferences
- Manual control over system behaviour

### Why it matters

Many systems operate with default or unmanaged behaviour, which often leads to suboptimal outcomes.
Flex:Schedule allows customers to quickly improve performance by aligning battery operation with real-world conditions and pricing structures.

### Example use case

A business operates under a time-of-use tariff with higher pricing in the evening.
Using Flex:Schedule, they configure:
- Battery charging between 10am and 2pm
- Battery discharging between 5pm and 8pm

### Result:

Reduced reliance on expensive grid electricity and improved solar utilisation.

### Outcome

- Immediate operational improvements
- Better alignment between energy use and pricing
- Increased value from existing assets

## Flex:Tariff


### Headline

Ensure you’re on the right tariff—based on how you actually use energy.

### High-level explainer

Flex:Tariff analyses your real energy consumption data against a catalogue of electricity retail tariffs to recommend the most cost-effective option for your business.

### Detailed explainer

Choosing the right electricity tariff is one of the simplest ways to improve energy economics, but it is often treated as a one-off decision or based on rough assumptions rather than real site behaviour.
Flex:Tariff is designed to do this properly. It uses the customer’s actual historical energy data, including interval consumption and, where relevant, export behaviour, to compare how different electricity tariff options would have performed in practice.
This means the recommendation is not based on generic assumptions or broad customer categories. It is based on how the site actually uses electricity.
The feature compares the customer’s load profile against a catalogue of available tariff structures, which may include flat tariffs, time-of-use tariffs, and more complex commercial arrangements. It calculates the likely cost outcome under each option and identifies which tariff is the best fit based on real usage.
What makes Flex:Tariff particularly powerful is that it goes beyond standard comparison tools. In addition to using real interval data, it supports the inclusion of custom commercial tariff offerings. This is important because many business customers do not fit neatly into publicly advertised retail plans. They may have negotiated pricing, non-standard demand structures, or bespoke retailer arrangements that need to be included in a true like-for-like comparison.
Because the analysis is tied to real usage data, Flex:Tariff can also continue to create value over time. As site behaviour changes—through electrification, operational change, solar adoption, or battery use—the best tariff may change too. Flex can help ensure that the tariff remains aligned with the site, rather than becoming outdated.

### Key capabilities

- Tariff comparison using real historical energy consumption data
- Use of interval data rather than generic estimates
- Comparison across a catalogue of electricity retail tariffs
- Support for time-of-use and more complex tariff structures
- Ability to add custom commercial tariff offerings into the comparison
- Financial ranking of tariff options based on real site behaviour
- Ongoing re-evaluation as usage patterns evolve

### Why it matters

Many businesses are on tariffs that are no longer the best fit for how they operate. That can result in avoidable cost, even if the site has already invested in solar, battery, or other energy improvements.
Flex:Tariff helps ensure that the commercial structure around the energy system is as well matched as the hardware itself. It makes tariff selection evidence-based, commercially grounded, and responsive to real operating conditions.
For customers, this can unlock immediate savings without changing infrastructure. For providers, it is another way the platform moves beyond monitoring and into active value creation.

### Example use case

A commercial customer is currently on a legacy electricity tariff that was selected before solar was installed and before operating hours changed.
Flex:Tariff analyses the customer’s actual interval data and compares the current plan against a range of alternative tariffs, including a custom commercial offering from a retailer.
The analysis shows that:
- The current tariff is no longer optimal
- A different time-of-use tariff would better match the site’s daily load pattern
- The custom commercial offer performs even better under the customer’s actual profile

### Result:

The customer can make a tariff decision based on evidence, with a clear view of the likely cost difference.

### Outcome

- Lower electricity costs through better tariff selection
- Smarter commercial decisions based on real data
- Support for both standard and bespoke tariff structures
- Continuous alignment between tariff choice and site behaviour
