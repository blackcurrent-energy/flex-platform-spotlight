01 — Flex:Assure

Know your system is performing as promised.

Flex:Assure gives you continuous validation that your solar and battery system is performing against the design forecast. It compares real-world generation against expected output — and contextualises any shortfall, whether the cause is weather, shading, or a genuine system fault. You get confidence every day after installation, not just on day one.

Why it matters

Without ongoing benchmarking, underperformance goes unnoticed for months. Flex:Assure catches it early — so you're not paying for a system that isn't working.

Solar Battery Performance Fault detection

On-farm example

Solar generation is down 14% on forecast. Flex:Assure attributes the shortfall to recorded cloud cover for the period — no fault found. Your Farmlands rep is automatically notified so nothing falls through the cracks.

02 — Flex:Schedule

Automate how your system runs — without complexity.

Flex:Schedule lets you set simple, rule-based schedules for when your system charges from solar, when it draws from the battery, and when high-load equipment like irrigation pumps or milk vat cooling run. Set it once, and the system runs to your rules — around the clock, without manual input.

Why it matters

Running high-draw loads at the wrong time of day costs more than it should. Scheduling puts you in control of when energy is used — so you're not paying peak rates for loads that don't need to run at peak times.

Battery Irrigation Dairy Automation

On-farm example

Battery charges from solar between 9am and 3pm. Irrigation pump is scheduled off during peak tariff hours (7–9am, 5–8pm). Milk vat cooling restarts at 11pm on overnight rate. All rules set in under five minutes.

03 — Flex:Submetering

See exactly where energy is going on your farm.

Flex:Submetering breaks your total energy consumption down by load — milking shed, irrigation, heat pumps, EV chargers, or any other circuit you want to monitor. Instead of one number at the boundary, you get a clear picture of what's using energy, when, and how much it's costing.

Why it matters

You can't reduce what you can't see. Submetering reveals which loads are driving your bill — and which parts of the farm have the most to gain from optimisation or scheduling.

Milking shed Irrigation EV charging Heat pumps

On-farm example

Irrigation is responsible for 61% of total farm energy use. The milking shed accounts for 27%. Flex:Submetering identifies irrigation as the primary target for scheduling and tariff optimisation — a finding that wasn't visible from the main meter alone.

04 — Flex:Reporting

Performance updates that keep you informed.

Flex:Reporting delivers regular performance summaries — generation, consumption, savings, and system health — in a clear format that's useful without being overwhelming. Reports can go to you, your accountant, or your Farmlands rep. No spreadsheets, no manual interpretation needed.

Why it matters

An energy investment needs to prove itself over time. Flex:Reporting creates the ongoing record that shows whether the system is delivering the return that was projected at installation.

Monthly reports Savings tracking ROI Automated

On-farm example

End-of-month report lands in your inbox: 4,812 kWh generated, $1,106 saved against grid import, system running at 98% of forecast. Your Farmlands rep gets the same report — so they're across performance without needing to call.

05 — Flex:Control

Manage farm devices from anywhere.

Flex:Control lets you remotely switch and manage devices across the farm — heat pumps, EV chargers, hot water systems, and more. Control is available from any device, at any time, with a full audit trail of every action taken. Whether you're in the cab or off-farm, the farm stays manageable.

Why it matters

Being able to act on what the data is showing you is what turns visibility into value. Flex:Control closes the loop — so you're not just watching energy, you're managing it.

Heat pumps EV chargers Hot water Remote access

On-farm example

You're at the sale yards when the forecast shows a cold front incoming. You turn the woolshed heat pumps on remotely — they're warm before you get back. Full audit log records the action, time, and who authorised it.

06 — Flex:Tariff

Make sure your tariff matches how you use energy.

Flex:Tariff analyses your actual farm load profile against available tariff structures — and shows you in dollar terms whether you're on the right plan. Farms with large irrigation loads, time-shifted dairy operations, or battery storage often have options they're not aware of. Flex:Tariff makes them visible.

Commercial impact

Tariff misalignment is one of the most common sources of avoidable cost on farm. Flex:Tariff identifies the gap and quantifies it — giving you and your Farmlands rep a clear basis to act.

Time-of-use Demand tariffs Tariff comparison Cost analysis

On-farm example

Flex:Tariff models the farm's irrigation load profile against three available plans. Switching to a time-of-use tariff and shifting irrigation outside peak hours shows a projected saving of $4,200 per year — against the current flat-rate plan.

07 — Flex:Insights

Turn farm energy data into decisions that save money.

Flex:Insights surfaces the patterns and anomalies in your farm's energy data — and translates them into clear, actionable recommendations. Instead of raw numbers, you get specific findings: which load is running at the wrong time, which piece of equipment is drawing more than expected, where the biggest savings opportunities sit.

Why it matters

Data without interpretation is noise. Flex:Insights does the analytical work — so you spend your time acting on findings, not digging through them.

Anomaly detection Recommendations Savings opportunities Load analysis

On-farm example

Flex:Insights flags that the irrigation pump is drawing 12% more than its rated load — consistent with a worn impeller. The finding saves a costly reactive repair by catching it during a planned maintenance window.

08 — Flex:Simulator

Model the return before you make the investment.

Flex:Simulator runs what-if scenarios on your farm's actual energy data. Want to know what adding a battery would do to your bill? What if you added 20 kW more solar? What would the payback look like at current power prices versus a projected 15% tariff increase? Simulator answers those questions before you commit capital.

Investment decision support

Farming is full of capital decisions that need to stack up over time. Flex:Simulator gives you and your Farmlands rep a shared, data-grounded basis for those conversations — not assumptions.

Battery sizing Solar expansion Payback modelling Scenario planning

On-farm example

Adding a 30 kWh battery: Simulator models $3,800/yr in additional savings on the current tariff, with a 7.9-year payback. At a 15% tariff increase scenario, payback shortens to 6.5 years.

09 — Flex:Optimisation

Automated energy management. Set it and let it work.

Flex:Optimisation moves beyond manual schedules to fully automated energy management — dynamically dispatching battery storage, adjusting controllable loads, and responding to real-time price signals to minimise cost without any ongoing input from you. The farm's energy runs itself.

Why it matters

Manual scheduling captures value when conditions are predictable. Optimisation captures value when they aren't — responding to cloud, grid price spikes, or unexpected load without requiring anyone to act.

Automated dispatch Spot price response Battery management Demand response

On-farm example

A grid price spike at 5pm triggers automatic battery discharge to cover milking shed load — avoiding peak import entirely. Cloud cover at 2pm prompts early battery charging from the grid at a low rate. No manual input required, all decisions logged.
